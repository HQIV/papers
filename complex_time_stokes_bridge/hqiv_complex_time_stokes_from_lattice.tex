\begin{abstract}
\noindent\textbf{Position.}
There is no prior HQIV publication that derives complexified temporal structure from the null
lattice and compares it to Jenny Lorraine Nielsen's complex-time Navier--Stokes program
\cite{NielsenSemitaNS2026,NielsenTUFT2026}.
The thermodynamics companion \cite{hqiv-thermo-arrow-paper} records discrete dissipation signs on a
$C_3$ heat graph but explicitly disclaims 3D Navier--Stokes.
The TUFT bridge \cite{hqiv-tuft-bridge-paper} imports Hopf/Beltrami spectral language for mass
generation, not holomorphic Leray solutions.
This note closes the gap at the \emph{algebraic} level: it derives HQIV's time-angle / lapse /
rapidity-phase stack from lattice data already present in Tier~1, proves a single-mode Wick
identification with a Stokes semigroup factor, and states precisely what would be required for
full equivalence with Nielsen--Semita's solution \cite{NielsenSemitaNS2026}.

\noindent\textbf{Result (Tier~I, machine-checked).}
On shell index $m$, auxiliary field $\phi$, and coordinate time $t$,
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*),leftmargin=2em]
\item the HQVM lapse $N=1+\Phi+\phi t$ is the real horizon term plus Newtonian potential;
\item complex time $t_{\mathbb C}=t+i\,\phi t$ splits real clock from time angle;
\item lattice phase $\exp\bigl(i\,\theta(m)\bigr)$ with
  $\theta=\phi t\,\delta\theta'(m)$ is the \emph{star/conjugate} partner of the Stokes factor
  $\exp(-\nu k(m)^2\, t_{\mathbb C})$ on the imaginary-time ray
  $t_{\mathbb C}=i\,\theta/(\nu k(m)^2)$;
\item for real $t>0$, that Stokes factor has norm strictly below unity (semigroup damping).
\end{enumerate}
These items are packaged in \leanfile{Hqiv/Physics/ComplexTimeStokesWickBridge.lean}.

\noindent\textbf{Honest scope.}
This paper does \textbf{not} claim global smoothness, Millennium Option~A/C, or that HQIV
``solves'' classical Navier--Stokes regularity---HQIV's ontology requires the \emph{discrete null
lattice} to arrive at numbers, and both TUFT and HQIV treat finite Hopf shells as fundamental.
It \textbf{does} prove (Tier~I) that the HQIV lapse-modified fluid PDE reduces to the TUFT
Beltrami--NS form on chart-mapped modes, and that complex-time Stokes factors are Wick-conjugate
partners of lattice phase data.
\end{abstract}

\subsection{What existed before this note}

The HQIV paper series already contains the ingredients of a complex-time story, scattered across

\item \textbf{Rapidity phase in the zeta layer:} \leanfile{Hqiv/Physics/OctonionicZeta.lean} and
  \leanfile{Hqiv/Physics/RapidityZetaPhaseBridge.lean} identify the zeta exponent with

\item \textbf{Hopf/Beltrami mining:} \cite{hqiv-tuft-bridge-paper} aligns TUFT contact spectra with
  HQIV shell readouts \cite{NielsenTUFT2026}.
\end{itemize}
None of these manuscripts, however, states the complex-time/Stokes bridge as a single derived chain,
nor compares it to Nielsen's standalone Navier--Stokes work
\cite{NielsenSemitaNS2026,NielsenBeltramiComplexTime2026}.

\subsection{What Nielsen--Semita claim (external benchmark)}

Nielsen and Semita \cite{NielsenSemitaNS2026} develop 3D incompressible Navier--Stokes on
$\mathbb R^3$ using the Hopf fibration $S^1\to S^3\to\mathbb{CP}^1$ as an analytic tool.
Their program has two temporal channels:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
\item \textbf{Real time:} spectral irreducibility of fiber modes $\{m^2\}$ and base modes
  $\{k(k+1)\}$ drives a finite-time cascade (Option~C in Fefferman's formulation);
\item \textbf{Complex time} with $\Re t>0$: Stokes semigroup damping
  $\exp(-\nu|k|^2 t)$ suppresses the cascade and yields global holomorphic regularity (partial
  Option~A), with real-time smoothness recovered away from an exceptional set on the real axis.
\end{itemize}
Related work by Nielsen on Beltrami Navier--Stokes in complex time
\cite{NielsenBeltramiComplexTime2026} sits in the same research line.

\subsection{What this paper adds}

We derive HQIV's temporal structure from the lattice upward, prove the Wick bridge in Lean, and
show \emph{functional PDE identity} with TUFT/Beltrami Navier--Stokes on finite Hopf modes---without
claiming continuum global smoothness.

\section{Lattice derivation of time angle and phase}
\label{sec:lattice}

\subsection{Shell temperature and auxiliary field}

On the HQIV temperature ladder \cite{hqiv-thermo-arrow-paper,hqiv-kirchhoff-paper},
\begin{equation}
  T(m)=\frac{1}{m+1},\qquad \phi(m)=\frac{2}{T(m)}=2(m+1).
  \label{eq:phi-ladder}
\end{equation}
The auxiliary scalar $\phi$ is not a fitted transport coefficient: it is fixed by shell
combinatorics shared with O--Maxwell and HQVM sectors \cite{hqiv-oct-action-paper}.

\subsection{Time angle as lapse horizon term}

\begin{definition}[HQVM time angle]
\label{def:time-angle}
For shell-level $\phi>0$ and coordinate time $t\in\mathbb R$,
\begin{equation}
  \delta\theta'_{\mathrm{time}}(\phi,t):=\phi\,t.
  \label{eq:time-angle}
\end{equation}
The ADM lapse imposed by the informational-energy axiom is
\begin{equation}
  N(\Phi,\phi,t)=1+\Phi+\delta\theta'_{\mathrm{time}}(\phi,t),
  \label{eq:lapse}
\end{equation}
with Newtonian potential $\Phi$.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Lapse decomposition (proved)]
\label{prop:lapse-decomp}
$N(\Phi,\phi,t)=1+\Phi+\mathrm{timeAngle}(\phi,t)$, and
$\mathrm{timeAngle}(\phi,0)=0$.
\end{proposition}

The module \leanfile{Hqiv/Geometry/HQVMetric.lean} proves monotonicity
$\partial_t\,\mathrm{timeAngle}=\phi>0$ and the first-period identity
$\mathrm{timeAngle}(\phi,2\pi/\phi)=2\pi$: time angle is read mod $2\pi$ as horizon phase
accumulation, not as an extra fitted parameter.

\subsection{Tipping angle and polar angle on shells}

The Maxwell tipping channel defines, for electric-energy proxy $E'$,
\begin{equation}
  \delta\theta'(E')=\arctan(E')\cdot\frac{\pi}{2}.
  \label{eq:delta-theta-prime}
\end{equation}
On shell index $m$, the \emph{polar angle} used in the zeta layer is
\begin{equation}
  \theta(m):=\mathrm{polarAngleFromRapidity}(\phi,t,m)
  =\phi\,t\,\delta\theta'(m).
  \label{eq:polar-angle}
\end{equation}
The bridge \leanfile{Hqiv/Physics/RapidityZetaPhaseBridge.lean} proves
\begin{equation}
  i\,\phi t\,\delta\theta'(m)
  = i\,\theta(m)
\end{equation}
as an exponent in $\mathbb C$, i.e.\ the zeta phase factor and the polar scaffold are the same
object.

\subsection{Complexified lattice time}

\begin{definition}[HQIV complex time (formal split)]
\label{def:complex-time}
\begin{equation}
  t_{\mathbb C}(\phi,t):=t+i\,\mathrm{timeAngle}(\phi,t)=t+i\,\phi t.
  \label{eq:hqiv-complex-time}
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Real and imaginary parts (proved)]
\label{prop:complex-parts}
$\Re t_{\mathbb C}=t$ and $\Im t_{\mathbb C}=\phi t$.
Moreover $\Im t_{\mathbb C}+1+\Phi=N(\Phi,\phi,t)$.
\end{proposition}

Thus HQIV already carries a complex-time \emph{coordinate}: real part = coordinate clock; imaginary
part = time angle entering the lapse.

\subsection{Rapidity-modulated zeta phase}

The shell term in the HQIV lattice zeta \cite{hqiv-oct-action-paper} includes
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal Z_m(\delta,\phi,t,s)
  =\mathrm{eff}(\delta,m)^{-s}\,
   \exp\!\bigl(i\,\phi t\,\delta\theta'(m)\bigr).
  \label{eq:zeta-term}
\end{equation}
The phase factor is unit modulus: it is oscillatory data on the discrete shell line, not dissipative
by itself.

\section{Effective fluid closure from the same scalars}
\label{sec:fluid}

The modified fluid scaffold \cite{hqiv-oct-action-paper} reuses $\phi$, $\Theta_{\mathrm{local}}$,
and $\dot\delta\theta'$:
\begin{align}
  f(a_{\mathrm{loc}},\phi) &= \frac{a_{\mathrm{loc}}}{a_{\mathrm{loc}}+\phi/6},
  \label{eq:inertia}\\
  \mathbf g_{\mathrm{vac}} &= -\frac{\gamma}{6}\nabla(\phi\,\dot\delta\theta'),
  \label{eq:vacuum}\\
  \nu_{\mathrm{eddy}} &= \gamma\,\Theta_{\mathrm{local}}\,|\dot\delta\theta'|\,\ell_{\mathrm{coh}}^2\,C.
  \label{eq:eddy}
\end{align}
Lapse-modified momentum (DNS/RANS scaffold) multiplies inertia by $N$ from
\eqref{eq:lapse} \cite{hqiv-oct-action-paper}.

\begin{remark}[Two dissipation channels]
The \emph{phase} channel \eqref{eq:zeta-term} is oscillatory ($|\exp(i\theta)|=1$).
The \emph{eddy-viscosity} channel \eqref{eq:eddy} is dissipative in real time through
$|\dot\delta\theta'|$.
Nielsen's complex-time regularity uses semigroup \emph{decay} on $\Re t>0$; HQIV routes analogous
dissipation through \eqref{eq:eddy}, not through the zeta phase.
\end{remark}

\section{Single-mode Stokes factor and the Wick bridge}
\label{sec:wick}

\subsection{Stokes semigroup factor}

\begin{definition}[Mode Stokes factor]
For viscosity $\nu>0$, mode eigenvalue $k^2>0$, and complex time $t_{\mathbb C}\in\mathbb C$,
\begin{equation}
  S_\nu(k^2,t_{\mathbb C}):=\exp\!\bigl(-\nu k^2 t_{\mathbb C}\bigr).
  \label{eq:stokes-factor}
\end{equation}
On the real axis with $t>0$, $|S_\nu(k^2,t)|<1$: strict damping.
\end{definition}

This is the local building block of Nielsen's complex-time Stokes semigroup analysis
\cite{NielsenSemitaNS2026}.

\subsection{HQIV phase factor}

\begin{definition}[HQIV shell phase factor]
For real angle $\theta$,
\begin{equation}
  P(\theta):=\exp(i\theta).
\end{equation}
On shell $m$, $P(\theta(m))$ is the zeta phase in \eqref{eq:zeta-term}.
\end{definition}

\subsection{Main Wick theorem (proved)}

\begin{theorem}[Wick bridge / conjugate identification]
\label{thm:wick}
Fix $\nu k^2\neq 0$ and real $\theta$.
Define imaginary Stokes time
\begin{equation}
  t_{\mathbb C}=\frac{i\,\theta}{\nu k^2}.
  \label{eq:imag-stokes-time}
\end{equation}
Then
\begin{equation}
  S_\nu(k^2,t_{\mathbb C})=\overline{P(\theta)}=\star P(\theta),
  \label{eq:wick-main}
\end{equation}
where $\star$ is complex conjugation.
Equivalently, $P(\theta)=\star S_\nu(k^2,t_{\mathbb C})$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Machine-checked as \leanid{stokesModeFactor_imaginaryTime_eq_hqivPhaseFactor_star} and
\leanid{hqivShellPhaseFactor_eq_stokes_star} in
\leanfile{Hqiv/Physics/ComplexTimeStokesWickBridge.lean}.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Shell specialization]
\label{cor:shell}
For each shell $m$ with $\theta(m)=\phi t\,\delta\theta'(m)$ and mode scale $k(m)^2>0$,
\begin{equation}
  \exp\!\bigl(i\,\phi t\,\delta\theta'(m)\bigr)
  =\star\,\exp\!\Bigl(-\nu k(m)^2\cdot\frac{i\,\theta(m)}{\nu k(m)^2}\Bigr).
\end{equation}
\end{corollary}

\begin{theorem}[Real-axis damping (proved)]
\label{thm:damp}
If $\nu,k^2,t>0$ are real, then $\|S_\nu(k^2,t)\|<1$.
\end{theorem}

\end{proof}

\section{Functional PDE identity with TUFT Beltrami--NS}
\label{sec:pde-functional}

\subsection{Shared finite-mode ontology}

Both programs reject a literal continuum as fundamental:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
\item \textbf{TUFT} \cite{NielsenTUFT2026}: finite Hopf shells $S^1\to S^{2n+1}\to\mathbb{CP}^n$,
  contact Beltrami spectrum, zeta-regularized sector data;
\item \textbf{HQIV}: discrete null-lattice shells $m\in\mathbb{N}$, lock-in horizon
  $\mathrm{referenceM}=4$, temperature ladder $T(m)=1/(m+1)$.
\end{itemize}
Numbers (masses, viscosities, mode budgets) are \emph{lattice/shell readouts}. Global smoothness of
classical 3D NS is therefore \textbf{not} the equivalence target; \textbf{operator-form coincidence}
on matched modes is.

\subsection{TUFT Beltrami--NS momentum form}

On a Beltrami field ($\nabla\times\mathbf u=\lambda\mathbf u$), Nielsen's incompressible NS
reduces to the schematic pointwise balance (continuum chart, no existence claim)
\begin{equation}
  \rho\bigl(\partial_t\mathbf u+(\mathbf u\cdot\nabla)\mathbf u\bigr)
  =-\nabla p+\nu_{\mathrm{tot}}\,\Delta\mathbf u+\mathbf f,
  \label{eq:tuft-beltrami-ns}
\end{equation}
with mode diffusivity tied to Hopf eigenvalues: fiber chart $\{m^2\}$, base chart $\{k(k+1)\}$, and
TUFT minimal coexact ladder $\lambda_{\min}(n)=n+1$ on winding sectors $n=1,2,3$
\cite{NielsenTUFT2026,hqiv-tuft-bridge-paper}.

\subsection{HQIV lapse-modified form and the classical limit}

HQIV's simulator axiom packages the modified balance \cite{hqiv-oct-action-paper}
\begin{equation}
  N\,\rho f\Bigl(\partial_t\mathbf u+(\mathbf u\cdot\nabla)\mathbf u\Bigr)
  =-\nabla p+\nu_{\mathrm{tot}}\,\Delta\mathbf u+\mathbf g_{\mathrm{vac}}+\mathbf f,
  \label{eq:hqiv-lapse-rans}
\end{equation}
with $N=1+\Phi+\phi t$, inertia $f(a_{\mathrm{loc}},\phi)$, vacuum source
$\mathbf g_{\mathrm{vac}}=-(\gamma/6)\nabla(\phi\,\dot\delta\theta')$, and
$\nu_{\mathrm{tot}}=\nu_{\mathrm{mol}}+\nu_{\mathrm{eddy}}$ as in \S\ref{sec:fluid}.

\begin{theorem}[Functional PDE identity at the Beltrami classical limit (proved)]
\label{thm:pde-functional}
When $N=1$, $f=1$, $\mathbf g_{\mathrm{vac}}=0$, and $\nu_{\mathrm{tot}}$ is matched, the HQIV
residual \eqref{eq:hqiv-lapse-rans} equals the TUFT Beltrami--NS residual \eqref{eq:tuft-beltrami-ns}
term-by-term.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Machine-checked as \leanid{tuftBeltramiNS_residual_eq_hqiv_classical_limit} and
\leanid{tuftBeltramiNS_component_iff_hqiv_classical_limit} in
\leanfile{Hqiv/Physics/TUFTBeltramiHQIVPDEBridge.lean}.
\end{proof}

Away from this limit, HQIV's lapse, inertia, and vacuum channels are \emph{extra physics} from the
null lattice; they collapse to TUFT's Beltrami--NS template when the chart is Beltrami-aligned and
gradients vanish.

\subsection{Correct statement of equivalence}

\begin{proposition}[Provable equivalence (single-mode Wick)]\label{prop:provable-eq}
For fixed $(\nu,k^2,\theta)$ with $\nu k^2\neq 0$, HQIV's shell phase factor and Nielsen's Stokes
semigroup factor are \textbf{Wick-conjugate partners} on the imaginary-time ray
\eqref{eq:imag-stokes-time}.
They are not identical as complex numbers unless $\theta\in\{0,\pi\}$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proposition}[Structural equivalence (dissipation channel)]
When $\dot\Theta$ is identified with $\delta\theta'(E')$ in the fluid chart, HQIV eddy viscosity
\eqref{eq:eddy} is proportional to $|\dot\Theta|$, providing a real-time dissipation slot
analogous to Nielsen's $\Re t>0$ damping, but \textbf{not} yet proved equal to a Navier--Stokes
viscosity coefficient.
\end{proposition}

time angle, lapse, and rapidity phase; machine-checked Wick and PDE-functional bridges to TUFT
Beltrami--NS; and an explicit refusal to conflate lattice physics with Millennium smoothness.

\leanid{TUFTBeltramiHQIVPDEFunctionalEquivalence} bundles.

Millennium Option~A/C as stated in \cite{NielsenSemitaNS2026}.
