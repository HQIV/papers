The present note states what the BBN Lean stack \emph{actually proves} about
primordial light-element readouts when lock-in $\eta$, nucleon masses, and
composite-trace binding weights are already fixed upstream.

At lock-in the library defines an $8\times8$ network readout for
$Y_p$, $D/H$, $^3\mathrm{He}/H$, and $^7\mathrm{Li}/H$ from valley
counts, Boltzmann weights, and cluster binding $Q$ values tied to

$\eta$ consistency (\hyperref[lean:bbn-full-vital]{full BBN vital readout}).
Reader-friendly anchors with clickable Lean links are in
Appendix~\ref{app:lean-index}.

\noindent\textbf{Single absolute anchor (quarantined).}
Dimensionful nucleon masses enter only through the proton lock-in witness
at $\mathrm{referenceM}=4$; lock-in $\eta_{\mathrm{paper}}$ is imported from
the baryogenesis witness layer, not refit here. Coc et al.\ semi-analytic

readout on the shell--temperature map, not a continuum-limit completion
programme. Full PRIMAT-style reaction networks, Li-problem resolution, and
sphaleron/chemical-potential transport remain comparison-layer or future

\noindent\textbf{Status.}

scaffold: $^7\mathrm{Li}/H$ from the weight ansatz can diverge at mid-epoch

\section{Scope and claim discipline}
\label{sec:scope}

The HQIV programme ties discrete null-shell counting, horizon ratios, and
octonionic-sector packaging \cite{ettinger2026hqiv,hqiv-so8-paper}. The
baryogenesis note \cite{hqiv-baryogenesis-paper} fixes how
$\eta(n;N)$ shares the same normalisation as $\Omega_k(n;N)$ at lock-in.
\textbf{This note answers: given that normalisation, what light-element
statements are already theorems in Lean, and what remains interpretive or
comparison-layer physics?}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
\item \textbf{Proved in Lean (network spine).}
  Valley counts for $A=2,3,4$; cluster binding $Q$ for deuterium and
  $^4\mathrm{He}$ aligned with composite-trace nucleon binding; neutron
  fraction and $Y_p=2x_n/(1+x_n)$ in $(0,1)$; $\eta$ at BBN equals
  $\eta_{\mathrm{paper}}$ (the \hyperref[lean:eta-bbn-eq]{BBN--$\eta$ identity});
  positivity of $D/H$, $^3\mathrm{He}/H$, $^7\mathrm{Li}/H$ readouts at
  lock-in (the \hyperref[lean:bbn-network-vital]{network vital readout}).
\item \textbf{Proved in Lean (epoch spine).}
  Shell index $m(T)=\lfloor T_{\mathrm{Pl,MeV}}/T\rfloor-1$; BBN mid-epoch
  shell strictly above lock-in and below today (the
  \hyperref[lean:bbn-mid-shell-order]{mid-epoch shell ordering});
  epoch readout positivity at $T\approx 0.1\,\mathrm{MeV}$ (the
  \hyperref[lean:bbn-epoch-vital]{epoch vital readout}).
\item \textbf{Proved in Lean (integrated witness).}
  Freeze-out $Y_p$ in the Cooke-style observed band; integrated $Y_p$
  within $1\%$ of freeze-out at exported numerics (the
  \hyperref[lean:bbn-integrated-vital]{integrated witness vital readout}).
\item \textbf{Comparison layer only.}
  Coc et al.\ \cite{coc2015} semi-analytic $Y_p(\eta,N_\nu,\tau_n)$ and
  $D/H(\eta)$ in the \hyperref[lean:bbn-umbrella]{BBN umbrella module}---not
  derived from HQIV weights.
\item \textbf{Not claimed.}
  Full 400-reaction PRIMAT network in Lean; Li-problem solution; independent
  refit of $\eta$ or nucleon masses; continuum transport of weak rates.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Network readout from valley weights}
\label{sec:network}

The \hyperref[lean:bbn-network]{network-from-weights module} encodes light
elements as a weighted readout on the same Fano/network picture used for
nucleon binding \cite{hqiv-tuft-sm-lagrangian-paper}:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
\item \textbf{Valley counts.}
  $A=2\mapsto 2$, $A=3\mapsto 4$, $A=4\mapsto 6$ (helium-4 valley).
\item \textbf{Boltzmann factor.}
  $\exp(-Q/T)$ at temperature $T$ (MeV) for each channel $Q$.
\item \textbf{Neutron sector.}
  $x_n=n/p$ from $\Delta m$ and $T$; $Y_p=2x_n/(1+x_n)$.
\item \textbf{Light ions.}
  $D/H\propto \eta_{10}^{-\alpha_D}\times$ thermal sink $\times$
  Boltzmann weight; analogous exponent slots for $^3\mathrm{He}$ and
  $^7\mathrm{Li}$.
\end{itemize}

At lock-in temperature the readout packages
$(\eta,Y_p,D/H,{}^3\mathrm{He}/H,{}^7\mathrm{Li}/H)$ with
$\eta=\eta_{\mathrm{paper}}$. The
\hyperref[lean:bbn-network-vital]{network vital readout} theorem bundles
positivity and the $\eta$ identity---this is the certificate-level content
of ``BBN from network weights,'' not a hand-tuned abundance table.

\begin{remark}[Epoch dependence]
Partition formulas evaluated at lock-in temperature are \emph{not} the same
as relic abundances after integration over the BBN window; Section~\ref{sec:epoch}

\end{remark}

\section{BBN as a cosmological epoch on the temperature ladder}
\label{sec:epoch}

The \hyperref[lean:bbn-epoch-evolution]{epoch evolution module} maps
temperature to shell index
\[
  m(T)+1 = T_{\mathrm{Pl,MeV}}/T,
\]
with a QCD onset shell, lock-in shell $m_{\mathrm{lockin}}=\mathrm{referenceM}$,
BBN window $T\in[0.01,1]\,\mathrm{MeV}$, and CMB today as the cold end of
the ladder. Proved ordering:
\[
  m_{\mathrm{lockin}} < m_{\mathrm{BBN,mid}} < m_{\mathrm{now}},
\]
where $m_{\mathrm{BBN,mid}}$ corresponds to $T\approx 0.1\,\mathrm{MeV}$.
Freeze-out internal temperature is defined consistently with neutron--proton
equilibrium break (the \hyperref[lean:bbn-freezeout-temp]{freeze-out
temperature identity}).

The \hyperref[lean:bbn-epoch-network]{epoch network module} supplies a
rate scaffold (weak, $np$, $dd$, $^3\mathrm{He}$ branches) and Hubble
dilution---schematic reaction bookkeeping for integration witnesses, not a
published PRIMAT clone in Lean.

\subsection{Dynamic $C_2$ lapse clock (BBN lab)}
\label{sec:dynamic-c2}

(\cite{hqiv-baryogenesis-paper}, \texttt{hqiv\_dynamic\_bulk\_bbn.py})

clock.  Matter curvature enters through $B_{\mathrm{curv}}(\xi)$ in the
$\kappa_6$ matter slot; \textbf{lapse concentration $C_2(\xi)$} is
applied in the deuterium bottleneck ($T\le\gamma w_{\mathrm{strong}}T_{\mathrm{freeze}}(\eta)$) via
\[
T_{\mathrm{bn}}(\eta)=\gamma\,w_{\mathrm{strong}}\,T_{\mathrm{freeze}}(\eta),
\quad
w_{C_2}(T)=\gamma\,w_{\mathrm{strong}}\,\frac{Q_D^{\mathrm{eff}}(T)}{Q_{np}},
\quad
f_{C_2}=(\kappa_{6,\mathrm{ref}}/\kappa_6)^{w_{C_2}}
\]
for $T\le T_{\mathrm{bn}}(\eta)$, with $\kappa_6=\eta_{\mathrm{paper}}\,B_{\mathrm{curv}}\,\gamma\,C_2(\xi)$
and Lean names \leanid{bbnDynamicC2BottleneckT\_MeV},
\leanid{bbnDynamicC2LapseExponent},
\leanid{bbnDynamicC2OpportunitySuppression}
(\leanfile{Hqiv/Physics/DynamicBBNBaryogenesis.lean}).
At the dynamic-$\eta$ witness ($\eta_{10}\approx 6.2$) this raises $D/H$ from

readout.

\emph{scaffold only} until a dedicated nuclear-network upgrade is proved.

\item Replacement of PRIMAT/MESA with the HQIV rate scaffold.

\item Resolution of the lithium problem without further nuclear-network work.
\item Any continuum-limit or patch-refinement programme.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}

readout} at lock-in, lifted to a \emph{cosmological epoch} on the
temperature--shell ladder, and closed by \emph{integrated abundance witnesses}

dynamic-$C_2$ integrator audit of \S\ref{sec:dynamic-c2}). Companion
