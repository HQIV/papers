\begin{abstract}
Heavy-flavour hadron and quarkonium decays are usually described with large
tables of fitted partial widths, current-quark masses, and channel-specific
form factors.
We extend the HQIV three-ledger decay structure---already validated on nuclear
$\beta$ tipping \cite{hqiv-nucleon-binding-paper}---to open and hidden
heavy-flavour states by generating decay channels from the same discrete
combinatorial rules that govern the lock-in mass spectrum and weak widths.
Branching ratios then follow by normalization, with no external partial-width
tables; open-flavour topology seeds carry unit prior, and their relative
competition comes only from derived ledger factors and phase space.

On the monogamy split $\gamma=2/5$, hidden vector quarkonia carry a leptonic
contact density $f_{\mathrm{EM}}=1/\gamma+1+\gamma=39/10$, a fixed consequence
of inverse-shell contact, unit vector-current normalisation, and second-order
Fano return on the discrete carrier.
With hadronic/radiative width pooling and the contact factor, the HQIV readout
gives $\mathcal{B}(J/\psi\to e^+e^-)\approx 5.99\%$, consistent with the
PDG branching $\mathcal{B}(J/\psi\to e^+e^-)\approx 5.9\%$
\cite{pdg2024,augustin1974,peskin1995,schwartz2014}; leading single-shell contact
alone predicts $\sim 1.7\%$, roughly $3.5\times$ below that reference.

The mass panel from pion through $\Upsilon(1S)$ uses the same quark
meta-resonance gaps as the TUFT synthesis \cite{hqiv-tuft-sm-lagrangian-paper}.
OZI suppression, CKM slot squares ($\gamma/8$, $\gamma/16$, $\gamma/32$), and
phase-space factors weight programmatically generated daughter combinations;
$\gamma$-derived production weights reproduce sensible $D$ vs.\ $B$ and on-shell
$\Upsilon$ hierarchies at LHC and Belle-scale kinematics.
A comparison discharge against PDG and facility references
\cite{pdg2024,belle2002,cms2018} spans $502$ open-channel readout rows and
$13$ curated diagnostic branching comparisons with Monte Carlo $\sigma$
propagation (Table~\ref{tab:branching-comparison}).
The structural suite has $80$ pass rows and zero failures; the broad readout
comparison has $70.0\%$ of directly referenced open-channel rows within $10\%$
of PDG and $92.3\%$ of the curated diagnostic rows within $20\%$.
Laboratory proton mass and all PDG tabulations \cite{pdg2024,codata2018} enter only
as comparison targets, never as prediction inputs.
These relations are machine-checked in the Lean formalization
(Appendix~\ref{app:lean}). Upstream of this note, the TUFT+SM synthesis also discharges

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Heavy-flavour physics sits at the intersection of strong confinement, electroweak
coupling, and collider phenomenology.
Quarkonia such as $J/\psi$ and $\Upsilon(1S)$ probe both QCD binding and
electromagnetic annihilation \cite{pdg2024,peskin1995,schwartz2014,augustin1974,herb1977};
open charm and bottom hadrons anchor weak-decay CP studies and $B$-factory
programs \cite{pdg2024,kobayashi1973,belle2002}.
In standard treatments each parent state carries a long list of partial widths
---often extracted from data or computed with model-dependent form factors
\cite{pdg2024,peskin1995,schwartz2014}---and
branching ratios are quoted as empirical inputs rather than derived outputs.

The HQIV programme already separates decay into three \emph{ledgers} on the
discrete null-lattice spine: \emph{(i)}~mass and kinematic endpoint $Q$,
\emph{(ii)}~curvature overlap on strong and OZI-suppressed channels,
\emph{(iii)}~weak width from $G_F$ and the Fano/Hopf bridge slot
\cite{hqiv-nucleon-binding-paper,hqiv-tuft-sm-lagrangian-paper}.
That separation fixed free-neutron $\beta$ width and light-isotope tipping
without half-life fit parameters.
\textbf{The present question is whether the same ledger discipline extends to
charm and bottom}---including hidden $Q\bar Q$ vector states---without
injecting PDG current-quark masses, MS\=bar inputs, or tabulated partial widths
\cite{pdg2024}.

We answer affirmatively at the readout level.
Masses for open and hidden heavy-flavour states follow from the quark
meta-resonance chart locked at $\mathrm{referenceM}=4$
\cite{hqiv-tuft-sm-lagrangian-paper}.
Decay channels are generated programmatically from discrete daughter pools and
ledger weights (OZI factors, $\gamma$-indexed CKM slots, phase-space priors);
partial widths sum within each ledger slot and branching ratios normalize to unity
by construction.
The strongest single physics result is the hidden-quarkonium electromagnetic
contact factor: on $\gamma=2/5$, leptonic $e^+e^-$ widths acquire
$1/\gamma+1+\gamma=39/10$ as a theorem of the monogamy bookkeeping, not an
adjustable multiplier.
That factor brings the HQIV leptonic readout into agreement with the PDG
branching $\mathcal{B}(J/\psi\to e^+e^-)\approx 5.9\%$ \cite{pdg2024,augustin1974},
whereas leading single-shell contact alone predicts $\sim 1.7\%$.

This note is positioned after the published TUFT+SM synthesis
\cite{hqiv-tuft-sm-lagrangian-paper} and the nucleon-binding three-ledger programme
\cite{hqiv-nucleon-binding-paper}.
Tier~0--1 supply the discrete spine

\subsection{Three ledgers on the discrete carrier}

HQIV treats each decay edge as readout on the same information budget that fixes

\subsection{Multichannel readout from discrete rules}

Conventional phenomenology often begins with a static branching table per parent.
Here, decay channels are \emph{generated} from combinatorial rules consistent
with the discrete carrier:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
\item \textbf{Daughter pools} --- pseudoscalar, vector, open-charm, open-bottom,
  and lepton-pair sectors allowed by quantum numbers on the patch chart.
\item \textbf{Kinematic filter} --- retain only combinations with $Q>0$ at HQIV
  masses.
\item \textbf{Ledger assignment} --- tag each open combination as strong, weak,
  electromagnetic, or weak-hadronic.
\item \textbf{Weighting} --- assign common topology seed weight, then apply OZI
  overlap, CKM slots, strangeness factors, derived topology contacts, and
  hadronic phase space $\propto Q^{3/2}$.
\item \textbf{Normalization} --- branching ratios are partial widths divided by
  the total; sums equal unity by construction when the total width is nonzero.
\end{enumerate}

This is a first-principles multichannel description in the patch sense: the
channel list is an \emph{output} of the same discrete spine that fixes masses,
not an imported table.
Light hadrons ($\Lambda\to p\pi$, $K\to\pi\ell\nu$) retain small topology
lists; open charm and bottom use topology seeds with the proved unit prior plus
derived contact factors (e.g.\ $2/77$, $10$, $7/2$, $25/4$), and quarkonium
parents use broad combinatorial expansion (Appendix~\ref{app:channels}).

The rational imprint $\alpha=3/5$ and monogamy split $\gamma=2/5$ enter every
weight; they are not per-channel knobs.
Comparison with PDG branching fractions \cite{pdg2024}, facility $\sqrt{s}$, and
production fractions is quarantined in a separate layer that never feeds back into
predictions.

\section{Mass gaps for open and hidden states}
\label{sec:mass}

Heavy-flavour masses are read out from the quark meta-resonance ladder, not from
current-quark inputs.
Up-type and down-type gaps $(m_c-m_u)$ and $(m_b-m_s)$ anchor open charm and
bottom sectors; the proton lock-in at $\mathrm{referenceM}=4$ supplies the
baryon reference \cite{hqiv-tuft-sm-lagrangian-paper}.

\paragraph{Open charm and bottom.}
Open charm mesons ($D$, $D_s$) add the up-type gap with heavy-flavour fraction
$\tfrac12(1+\gamma/4)$.
Charmed baryons ($\Lambda_c$, \ldots) combine the proton witness with charm and
optional strangeness lifts $(m_K-m_\pi)$.
Open bottom mesons ($B$) anchor on the bottom meta-resonance mass with a
proton--pion offset; bottom baryons stack charm and strangeness similarly.

\paragraph{Hidden quarkonia.}

\begin{equation}

\begin{equation}

\section{Decay structure and multichannel expansion}
\label{sec:decay}

\subsection{OZI and hadronic pooling}

Hadronic partial widths on the curvature ledger carry OZI/Zweig suppression
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{OZI}}(n_V) = \frac{\gamma}{4}\left(1 + \frac{\gamma\, n_V}{8}\right),
\end{equation}
with $n_V$ counting accessible vector modes in the daughter pool.
Hidden-quarkonium decays to light hadrons are strongly suppressed; transitions
to open charm or bottom pairs are less so.
For vector quarkonia, hadronic and radiative partial widths \emph{pool} before
branching normalization---otherwise a single $(\gamma)$ channel would spuriously
dominate the total width and drain leptonic branching.

Strong two-body topologies such as $\Delta(1232)\to p\pi$ and weak strange-baryon
decays ($\Lambda\to p\pi^-$) use the same ledger separation as in the light-hadron
regime; the weak branch $\mathcal{B}(\Lambda\to p\pi^-)\approx 0.64$ matches the
PDG reference $0.639$ \cite{pdg2024} without a dedicated fit.

\subsection{Why programmatic expansion}

The number of kinematically open channels grows rapidly for heavy parents.
Rather than extending a fitted branching table, HQIV enumerates allowed daughter
combinations and assigns each edge a ledger weight.
The result is a decay \emph{graph}---mass-budget edges weighted by OZI, CKM, and
phase space---from which cascade observables (inclusive branching, multi-step
chains at collider energy) are read off.

This is more constrained than a general effective field theory treatment:
every weight is a rational function of $\gamma$ already fixed by horizon
monogamy \cite{hqiv-3d-growth-paper}.
The trade-off is explicit: the proved object is not a continuum-QCD theorem but a
finite-patch factorization certificate.  Inclusive $B$ channels factor into hard,
jet, soft, bottom-rung, and first-return NLO ledger slots, with the fixed multiplier
\begin{equation}
  K^{B}_{\mathrm{NLO,ledger}} = 1+\frac{\gamma}{8} = \frac{21}{20}.
\end{equation}
The resulting multichannel readout remains testable against selected laboratory
channels while keeping partial-width tables and open-flavour relative seed
priors out of the prediction path.

Appendix~\ref{app:channels}.

\section{Quarkonium leptonic widths}
\label{sec:quarkonium-em}

Vector quarkonia couple to lepton pairs through the electromagnetic ledger
\cite{peskin1995,schwartz2014,pdg2024}.
On a compact $Q\bar Q$ carrier the naive QED vector-current estimate counts
only the lowest shell contact.
The HQIV monogamy bookkeeping adds two further terms already present elsewhere
on the discrete spine:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
\item \textbf{Inverse-shell contact} $1/\gamma$ --- overlap cost of accessing the
  hidden flavour shell against the monogamy budget;
\item \textbf{Unit normalisation} $+1$ --- standard vector-current counting at
  leading contact;
\item \textbf{Second-order Fano return} $+\gamma$ --- curvature round-trip on the
  weak Fano chart at second order.
\end{itemize}

The leptonic width multiplier is therefore not a fit parameter but a theorem
on the carrier:
\begin{equation}
  \label{eq:em-contact}
  f_{\mathrm{EM}} = \frac{1}{\gamma} + 1 + \gamma.
\end{equation}
With $\gamma=2/5$, Eq.~\eqref{eq:em-contact} yields
\leancert{$f_{\mathrm{EM}}=39/10$ exactly}{hep-decay-readout}{1}{hiddenQuarkoniumEMContactFactor_eq_thirtynine_tenths}.

\paragraph{Effect on $J/\psi\to e^+e^-$.}
At leading single-shell contact the HQIV readout gives
$\mathcal{B}(J/\psi\to e^+e^-)\approx 1.7\%$, roughly $3.5\times$ below the
PDG reference $\approx 5.9\%$ \cite{pdg2024,augustin1974}.
Pooling hadronic and radiative sectors removes the spurious single-$\gamma$
dominance that would otherwise drain leptonic branching; applying
Eq.~\eqref{eq:em-contact} raises the electromagnetic partial width by $39/10$
with no fitted leptonic-width input.
The combined HQIV readout gives
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{B}(J/\psi\to e^+e^-) \approx 5.99\%,
\end{equation}
in agreement with the PDG reference \cite{pdg2024,augustin1974} at the quoted
tolerance (Section~\ref{sec:results}).

The same factor applies to $\Upsilon\to e^+e^-$ and related vector-quarkonium
electromagnetic channels; bottomonium cascade branching ($\Upsilon\to J/\psi$)
remains a known refinement target (Section~\ref{sec:discussion}).

\section{Production ordering at collider kinematics}
\label{sec:production}

Absolute cross sections are not derived here; instead HQIV exports
$\gamma$-indexed \emph{production weights} and kinematic access predicates at
facility $\sqrt{s}$.

Open charm carries weight $\gamma/4$; open bottom carries $\gamma/8$.
These are proved to satisfy $\gamma/8<\gamma/4$, so bottom production is
suppressed relative to charm at the same ledger level---matching the qualitative
hierarchy seen at LHC and fixed-target energies \cite{cms2018,herb1977,pdg2024}.

On-shell thresholds distinguish which states are kinematically accessible:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
\item LHC $pp$ at $\sqrt{s}=13\,\mathrm{TeV}$ \cite{cms2018,pdg2024} --- $J/\psi$,
  $\Upsilon$, $D$, $B$, and charmed baryons above threshold;
\item SPS $400\,\mathrm{GeV}$ fixed target \cite{herb1977,pdg2024} --- charm
  threshold, bottom off-shell;
\item Belle-scale $\Upsilon(4S)$ \cite{belle2002,pdg2024} --- on-shell bottomonia
  with $\Upsilon$ dominating the on-shell bottom sector over $B$ mesons at HQIV
  masses.
\end{itemize}

Rate-ordering witnesses (e.g.\ $D$-over-$B$ fraction at LHC, $\Upsilon$ dominance
at Belle-scale energies) pass consistency checks without importing measured
cross-section tables into the prediction path.
These ordering statements follow from the same $\gamma$ ladder that weights decays.

  F_{\mathrm{collider}}

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{Relation to continuum QCD.}
OZI suppression and confinement language discharge through curvature overlap on
the discrete carrier \cite{hqiv-gluon-curvature-note}, not through fitted
potentials or MS\=bar current-quark masses.
The present readout is therefore complementary to lattice QCD and factorization
theorems: it asks what a zero-knob discrete spine predicts when branching is
\emph{derived} rather than imported.
For a direct continuum comparison, naive/factorized weak-decay treatments write
schematically
\[
  \Gamma(B\to D\pi)
    \propto |V_{cb}|^2\,a_i^2\,f_\pi^2\,|F_0^{B\to D}|^2\,p,
\]
with an external-emission coefficient $a_1$ for charged spectator modes and a
colour-suppressed coefficient $a_2$ for neutral modes
\cite{peskin1995,schwartz2014}.
The HQIV ledger already reproduces the charged external-contact row
$B^+\to D^0\pi^+$ at $46.34\%$ versus the $45\%$ comparison target, but it
under-reads the neutral $B^0\to D^0\pi^0$ channel.
In continuum language this is exactly the channel where colour-suppressed
factorization and final-state rescattering are normally separated from the
external-emission topology; in HQIV terms it points to a missing neutral
spectator curvature contact, not to a fitted partial-width input.

\paragraph{Discharged finite-ledger flavour/QCD items.}
At the patch layer, the three-generation CKM probability ledger assembled from
second-order Fano rungs has row and column sums equal to one\footnote{Lean:

Appendix~\ref{app:lean}.}.

\begin{equation}
  \Delta_{\mathrm{CP}}^{\mathrm{Fano}}
    = \frac{\gamma}{8}-\frac{\gamma}{32}
    = \frac{3}{80} > 0,
\end{equation}
not as a fitted CKM phase\footnote{Lean:
\leanid{cpOddFanoHolonomySkew_eq_three_over_eighty}; module~\leanmodfn{hep-decay-readout}{1};
Appendix~\ref{app:lean}.}.
Inclusive $B$ readouts use the factorized finite-patch NLO ledger weight\footnote{Lean:
\leanid{inclusiveBDecayFactorizedWeight_eq},
\leanid{inclusiveBNLOLedgerFactor_eq_twentyone_over_twenty}; module~\leanmodfn{hep-decay-readout}{1};
Appendix~\ref{app:lean}.}.

\paragraph{What remains open.}
Half-life comparisons use log-scale envelopes appropriate to order-of-magnitude
width agreement, not spectral line shapes.
Smooth continuum factorization, if desired for comparison with textbook QCD, is a
translation problem from the finite ledger rather than an extra input to the HQIV
prediction path.

\paragraph{Collider phenomenology.}
The decay graph formalism supports multi-step chains at declared facility
energy---for example $B\to J/\psi X \to e^+e^- X$---without refitting intermediate
widths.
Production ordering from $\gamma$ weights gives a falsifiable prior on relative
charm vs.\ bottom yields before absolute cross sections are modeled.
As the Fano-cycle mixing readout extends, the same ledger wiring should extend
to rare decays and mixing-dependent channels without introducing new fit sectors.

\section{Conclusion}

We extend the HQIV three-ledger decay structure to open and hidden heavy-flavour
states.
Masses follow the quark meta-resonance chart; channels are generated from discrete
daughter pools and ledger weights; branching ratios normalize without external
partial-width tables or open-flavour relative seed priors.
The hidden-quarkonium contact factor $1/\gamma+1+\gamma=39/10$ predicts
$\mathcal{B}(J/\psi\to e^+e^-)\approx 5.99\%$, consistent with the PDG reference
\cite{pdg2024,augustin1974}.
Comparison against PDG and facility references \cite{pdg2024,belle2002,cms2018}
spans $502$ open-channel readout rows and $13$ diagnostic branching comparisons with
Monte Carlo $\sigma$ propagation (Tables~\ref{tab:branching-comparison}
and~\ref{tab:readout-error-distribution}).
Formal certificates and reproducibility notes are in the appendices.

\section{Channel generation rules}
\label{app:channels}

Decay channels are generated programmatically from the same discrete rules
described in Section~\ref{sec:decay}.

\paragraph{Algorithm.}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
\item Form daughter pools: pseudoscalar ($\pi$, $K$, $\eta$), vector ($\rho$,
  $\omega$, $\phi$), open charm ($D$, $D_s$, $\Lambda_c$, \ldots), open bottom
  ($B$, $\Lambda_b$, \ldots), lepton pairs ($e^+e^-$, $\mu^+\mu^-$).
\item Enumerate combinations consistent with quantum-number tags on the parent.
\item Discard kinematically closed channels ($Q\le 0$).
\item Assign ledger tag and partial-width prior (OZI, CKM slot, $Q^{3/2}$ phase
  space for hadronic modes).
\item Normalize to branching ratios per parent.
\end{enumerate}
