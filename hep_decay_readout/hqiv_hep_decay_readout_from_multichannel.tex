\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Heavy-flavour physics sits at the intersection of strong confinement, electroweak
coupling, and collider phenomenology.
Quarkonia such as $J/\psi$ and $\Upsilon(1S)$ probe both QCD binding and
electromagnetic annihilation \cite{pdg2024,peskin1995,schwartz2014,augustin1974,herb1977};
open charm and bottom hadrons anchor weak-decay CP studies and $B$-factory
programs \cite{pdg2024,kobayashi1973,belle2002}.
In standard treatments each parent state carries a long list of partial widths
---often extracted from data or computed with model-dependent form factors
\cite{pdg2024,peskin1995,schwartz2014}---and
branching ratios are quoted as empirical inputs rather than derived outputs.

The HQIV programme already separates decay into three \emph{ledgers} on the
discrete null-lattice spine: \emph{(i)}~mass and kinematic endpoint $Q$,
\emph{(ii)}~curvature overlap on strong and OZI-suppressed channels,
\emph{(iii)}~weak width from $G_F$ and the Fano/Hopf bridge slot
\cite{hqiv-nucleon-binding-paper,hqiv-tuft-sm-lagrangian-paper}.
That separation fixed free-neutron $\beta$ width and light-isotope tipping
without half-life fit parameters.
\textbf{The present question is whether the same ledger discipline extends to
charm and bottom}---including hidden $Q\bar Q$ vector states---without
injecting PDG current-quark masses, MS\=bar inputs, or tabulated partial widths
\cite{pdg2024}.

\subsection{Unified spine discharge law}
\label{sec:spine-discharge}

Species-specific branching tables are replaced at the contact layer by a single
multiplicative law on \emph{ledger observables} extracted from each decay edge
(parent patch, channel tag, daughter discharge list):
\begin{equation}
  \label{eq:spine-discharge}
  W(\mathrm{parent},\mathrm{channel},\mathrm{daughters})
  = \prod_k g_k^{\,e_k(\mathrm{obs})},
  \qquad e_k\in\{0,1\},\quad g_k^0\equiv 1.
\end{equation}
Inactive generators contribute unity; no ``if $K^+$ then \ldots'' table multiplies
the physics.

\begin{definition}[Spine discharge observables]
\label{def:spine-observables}
For each edge $(\mathrm{parent},\mathrm{channel},\mathrm{daughters})$ define the
observable vector $\mathrm{obs}$ with binary exponents
$e_k(\mathrm{obs})\in\{0,1\}$ and fixed generators $g_k$ from the light-sector
slot table or heavy-flavour registry $\mathcal{G}_{\mathrm{contact}}$
(Definition~\ref{def:contact-registry}).
Inactive slots satisfy $g_k^{\,0}\equiv 1$.
\end{definition}

\label{def:double-monogamy}

\begin{equation}

\begin{theorem}[Light-sector spine uniqueness on the fixed registry]
\label{thm:spine-uniqueness}

\subsection{OZI and hadronic pooling}

Hadronic partial widths on the curvature ledger carry OZI/Zweig suppression
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{OZI}}(n_V) = \frac{\gamma}{4}\left(1 + \frac{\gamma\, n_V}{8}\right),
\end{equation}
with $n_V$ counting accessible vector modes in the daughter pool.
Hidden-quarkonium decays to light hadrons are strongly suppressed; transitions
to open charm or bottom pairs are less so.
For vector quarkonia, hadronic and radiative partial widths \emph{pool} before
branching normalization---otherwise a single $(\gamma)$ channel would spuriously
dominate the total width and drain leptonic branching.

\begin{lemma}[Branching normalization]
\label{lem:branching-normalization}
For partial widths $\{\Gamma_i\}$ with total $\Gamma=\sum_i\Gamma_i\neq 0$,
branching ratios $\mathcal{B}_i=\Gamma_i/\Gamma$ satisfy
$\sum_i\mathcal{B}_i=1$
(\leanid{branchingRatios_sum_one};
\leanfile{Hqiv/Physics/HepDecayReadout.lean}).
The multichannel readout applies this normalization after ledger weighting and
phase-space priors; no per-parent branching table is fitted.
\end{lemma}

Strong two-body topologies such as $\Delta(1232)\to p\pi$ use the same ledger
separation as in the light-hadron regime.
Weak strange-baryon decays ($\Lambda\to p\pi^-$) use the isospin-half neutral-pion
outlet on the property-spanning certificate with light-baryon neutral aperture
$(1-\gamma)/(1-\gamma/2-\gamma/12)=18/23$; the dynamic multichannel readout
reads $\mathcal{B}(\Lambda\to p\pi^-)\approx 64.9\%$ and
$\mathcal{B}(\Lambda\to n\pi^0)\approx 35.1\%$ versus PDG $63.9\%$ and $35.8\%$
($0.64\sigma$ and $0.40\sigma$ on the isospin-half readout).

combinations and assigns each edge a ledger weight.

This is more constrained than a general effective field theory treatment:
every weight is a rational function of $\gamma$ already fixed by horizon

finite-patch factorization certificate.  Inclusive $B$ channels factor into hard,

\begin{equation}

The resulting multichannel readout remains testable against selected laboratory

\section{Quarkonium leptonic widths}
\label{sec:quarkonium-em}

only the lowest shell contact.

on the discrete spine:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
\item \textbf{Inverse-shell contact} $1/\gamma$ --- overlap cost of accessing the

\item \textbf{Unit normalisation} $+1$ --- standard vector-current counting at
  leading contact;
\item \textbf{Second-order Fano return} $+\gamma/2$ --- half-strength curvature round-trip
  on the weak Fano chart at second order (full $+\gamma$ overshoots leptonic
  branching once hadronic sectors pool).
\end{itemize}

\begin{equation}

\end{equation}

\section{Production ordering at collider kinematics}
\label{sec:production}

Absolute cross sections are not derived here; instead HQIV exports
$\gamma$-indexed \emph{production weights} and kinematic access predicates at
facility $\sqrt{s}$.

Open charm carries weight $\gamma/4$; open bottom carries $\gamma/8$.
These are proved to satisfy $\gamma/8<\gamma/4$, so bottom production is
suppressed relative to charm at the same ledger level---matching the qualitative
hierarchy seen at LHC and fixed-target energies \cite{cms2018,herb1977,pdg2024}.

On-shell thresholds distinguish which states are kinematically accessible:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
\item LHC $pp$ at $\sqrt{s}=13\,\mathrm{TeV}$ \cite{cms2018,pdg2024} --- $J/\psi$,
  $\Upsilon$, $D$, $B$, and charmed baryons above threshold;
\item SPS $400\,\mathrm{GeV}$ fixed target \cite{herb1977,pdg2024} --- charm
  threshold, bottom off-shell;
\item Belle-scale $\Upsilon(4S)$ \cite{belle2002,pdg2024} --- on-shell bottomonia
  with $\Upsilon$ dominating the on-shell bottom sector over $B$ mesons at HQIV
  masses.
\end{itemize}

Rate-ordering witnesses (e.g.\ $D$-over-$B$ fraction at LHC, $\Upsilon$ dominance
at Belle-scale energies) pass consistency checks without importing measured
cross-section tables into the prediction path.
These ordering statements follow from the same $\gamma$ ladder that weights decays.

\paragraph{Relation to continuum QCD factorization.}
OZI suppression and confinement language discharge through curvature overlap on
the discrete carrier \cite{hqiv-gluon-curvature-note}, not through fitted
potentials or MS\=bar current-quark masses.
The present readout is therefore complementary to lattice QCD and factorization
theorems: it asks what a zero-knob discrete spine predicts when branching is
read out combinatorially rather than imported.
For a direct continuum comparison, naive/factorized weak-decay treatments write
schematically
\[
  \Gamma(B\to D\pi)
    \propto |V_{cb}|^2\,a_i^2\,f_\pi^2\,|F_0^{B\to D}|^2\,p,
\]
with an external-emission coefficient $a_1$ for charged spectator modes and a
colour-suppressed coefficient $a_2$ for neutral modes
\cite{peskin1995,schwartz2014}.
The HQIV ledger reproduces the charged external-contact row
$B^+\to D^0\pi^+$ at $\approx 45.5\%$ versus the $45\%$ comparison target
($0.11\sigma$).
Neutral colour-suppressed $B^0\to D^0\pi^0$ reads at $\approx 15.6\%$
versus $16\%$ once the bottom-neutral spectator $(5/3)(1-\gamma/4)=3/2$ is
assigned to the exact $(D^0,\pi^0)$ tag only
(\leanid{openFlavourContactWeight\_bottomNeutralSpectator};
\leanid{isNeutralBottomSpectator}).
Open-charm $B^0\to D^+\pi^0$ and related finite-completion modes carry the
default weak-bridge aperture $1/45$, not the $5/3$ complement.
In continuum language, colour-suppressed factorization and final-state rescattering
remain comparison-layer translations; in HQIV terms the neutral outlet required a
distinct curvature contact on the ledger, not a fitted partial-width input.
Final-state rescattering and higher-generation topology completion are tracked as
open spine items (Appendix~\ref{app:spine-gaps}).

\section{Channel generation rules}
\label{app:channels}

Decay channels are generated programmatically from patch-ledger predicates
(Section~\ref{sec:decay}) and property-classified weak outlets
(\texttt{hqiv\_property\_channels.py}, \texttt{hqiv\_property\_spanning.py}).

\paragraph{Algorithm.}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
\item Form daughter pools from species ledgers (charge, $S$, $C$, $B$, baryon number).
\item Apply weak/strong conservation and sparse spanning rules (single-$W$ load,
  oriented kaon outlets, minimal $K\pi$ / $3\pi$ tags).
\item Enumerate $n$-body combinations ($n=1$--$3$; caps $256$/$120$ guard combinatorics).
\item Discard kinematically closed channels ($Q\le 0$).
\item Assign \emph{contact seed} from spine discharge weight
  (\S\ref{sec:spine-discharge}); apply phase space
  and CKM once in the width layer.
\item Normalize to branching ratios per parent.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Type~A listed-pull audit.}

\paragraph{Half-life panel and width MC.}
Lifetime witnesses omit $n_\sigma$ not because dressing is ignored---predictions are
fully dressing-aware via $f_{\mathrm{weak}}$ and $f_{\mathrm{out}}$
(Table~\ref{tab:half-life-dressing})---but because propagating width MC $\sigma$ to
$t_{1/2}=\ln 2/\Gamma$ without a quarantined PDG $\sigma_{\mathrm{ref}}$ would
mis-report $10^6$--$10^8\sigma$ numbers that are export artefacts, not experimental
precision claims.
The honest comparison class is $\log_{10}|\mathrm{pred}/\mathrm{ref}|$ within declared
envelopes, aligned with the bottle/beam readout in the nucleon $\beta$ paper
\cite{hqiv-nucleon-binding-paper}.

\subsection{Outside-curvature overlay calculator}
\label{sec:outside-curvature-calculator}

Portable reader tool: \repath{scripts/hqiv_outside_curvature_calculator.py}
(mirrored in \repath{papers/hep_decay_readout/scripts/}; stdlib + local \nolinkurl{hqiv_*.py} only).
It composes Earth-surface $K_{\mathrm{mass\,chart}}$ (gravity, lab temperature, CMB dipole
$v/c$ from \repath{data/earth_outside_closure.json}) with facility
$K_{\mathrm{facility}}$ from Eq.~(\ref{eq:collider-width-factor}) or the LEP line-shape chart.

\paragraph{Inputs (CLI or JSON).}
Magnetic field $B$ (T), reference $B_{\mathrm{ref}}$ (default $4$\,T), comoving stream
fraction $f_{\mathrm{stream}}$, optional beam speed $v$ (adds $\sqrt{f_{\mathrm{stream}}^2+(v/c)^2}$),
line-shape stream fraction ( $e^+e^-$ factories), dressing chart
(\texttt{collider\_universal}, \texttt{collider\_native}, \texttt{line\_shape}),
kinematic exponent $n_{\mathrm{kin}}$ (default $\gamma/5$ for native LHC charts),
lab temperature, and gravity tier (\texttt{none}--\texttt{full}).

\paragraph{Outputs.}
$K_{\mathrm{mass\,chart}}$, $K_{\mathrm{facility}}$, $K_{\mathrm{total}}$,
$m_{\mathrm{apparent}}=m_{\mathrm{pole}}\,K_{\mathrm{total}}$, and combined pulls
\begin{equation}
  n_\sigma=
  \frac{|m_{\mathrm{ref}}-m_{\mathrm{compare}}|}
  {\sqrt{\sigma_{\mathrm{ref}}^2+\sigma_{\mathrm{dress}}^2}},
\end{equation}
where $m_{\mathrm{compare}}$ is either the HQIV pole (\texttt{earth\_tabulated} frame,
for PDG table comparisons) or $m_{\mathrm{apparent}}$ (\texttt{facility\_apparent} frame,
when the reference measurement was taken in a declared collider environment).
Default $\sigma_{\mathrm{dress}}$ propagates $\pm 5\%$ on $B$ and $\pm 10\%$ on
$f_{\mathrm{stream}}$ through $K_{\mathrm{facility}}$; override via JSON if your
experiment publishes facility systematics.

\paragraph{Commands.}
\begin{verbatim}
cd papers/hep_decay_readout/scripts && export PYTHONPATH=.
python3 hqiv_outside_curvature_calculator.py presets
python3 hqiv_outside_curvature_calculator.py dressing \
  --preset cms_lhc --json-out /tmp/dressing.json
python3 hqiv_outside_curvature_calculator.py compare \
  --hqiv-mev 2286.46 --reference-mev 2286.46 --sigma-mev 0.14 \
  --preset cms_lhc --frame facility_apparent
python3 hqiv_outside_curvature_calculator.py panel \
  --magnetic-field-tesla 3.8 --stream-fraction 0.12 \
  --chart collider_native --json-out /tmp/panel_facility.json
python3 hqiv_outside_curvature_calculator.py batch \
  --input ../../data/outside_curvature_calculator_example.json
\end{verbatim}
Example batch JSON: \texttt{data/outside\_curvature\_calculator\_example.json}.
The \texttt{panel} subcommand re-scores all $85$ excited-state PDG rows with both
earth-pole and facility-apparent $n_\sigma$ columns for apples-to-apples tension tables.
