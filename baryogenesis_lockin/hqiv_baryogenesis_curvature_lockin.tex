window'': it is \emph{not} proved from washout maximisation in Lean; it is
the packaged combinatorial stage on which curvature ratios are evaluated.

\section{Horizon ratios \(\Omega_k\) and lock-in calibration}

Define the cumulative partial integral
\[
I(n) \;:=\; \sum_{m=0}^{n-1} |\sigma(m)|
\]
(the \hyperref[lean:curvature-integral]{curvature integral}). For horizons
$N$ with $I(N)>0$, the library defines
$\Omega_k(n;N):=I(n)/I(N)$. At the lock-in shell, Lean proves positivity
$I(m_{\mathrm{lockin}})>0$ and the calibration
$\Omega_k(m_{\mathrm{lockin}};m_{\mathrm{lockin}})=1$ (the
\hyperref[lean:omega-k-lockin]{lock-in $\Omega_k$ calibration}). This is
the precise sense in which ``spatial curvature lock-in'' is a
\textbf{theorem}: normalisation of the discrete imprint integral at the
declared reference horizon, not an experimental input.

\begin{proposition}[Monotonicity and approach to lock-in, Lean]
\label{prop:mono-lockin}
Assume $I(N)>0$. Then for all $n_1\le n_2$,
$\Omega_k(n_1;N)\le \Omega_k(n_2;N)$, with strict inequality when
$n_1<n_2$ (the \hyperref[lean:omega-k-mono]{horizon-ratio monotonicity
pack}). If additionally $n\le N$, then $\Omega_k(n;N)\le 1$. In
particular, along shells $n\le m_{\mathrm{lockin}}$ with horizon
$N=m_{\mathrm{lockin}}$, the ratio $\Omega_k(n;m_{\mathrm{lockin}})$
increases strictly toward $1$ as $n$ approaches $m_{\mathrm{lockin}}$
from below.
\end{proposition}

The first-principles content formalised today is:
\begin{enumerate}

  This scaffold ties CP-odd increments to the same curvature-ratio channel used for \(\mathrm{Spin}(8)\) closure and measurement; it does \emph{not} derive the bias itself from non-associativity of the octonion product (that remains a Tier-IV target).
\item a GR/O--Maxwell compatibility package tying \((\alpha,\phi)\) across sectors.
\end{enumerate}
That package is already enough for a mathematically disciplined
``baryogenesis stands on the same shoulders as $\mathrm{Spin}(8)$
closure'' paper. The natural next formal manuscript in the programme is
the BBN stack above; the remaining distance to textbook electroweak
baryogenesis beyond that is washout, sphaleron integration, and
associator-derived CP violation---yield-level dynamics not yet in the
Lean corpus.
