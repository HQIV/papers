\section{Weak width and $G_F$}
\label{sec:weak-width}

The weak width slot uses the existing tipping scaffold
(\hyperref[lean:beta-decay-rate]{beta decay rate}) with coupling from
\hyperref[lean:gf-from-beta]{G\_F\_from\_beta}:
\[
  \Gamma_{\beta^-} \propto G_F^2\, m_e^5\, |\mathcal{M}|^2\, f_{\mathrm{geom}}\,/\hbar,
  \qquad
  |\mathcal{M}|^2 = \Bigl(\frac{\mathrm{residual}}{m_e}\Bigr)^4.
\]
This wiring is proved as slot identities in
\hyperref[lean:weak-width-slot]{weak width slot theorems}; it does not assert that
the overlap residual equals the PDG $\beta$ matrix element---only that the named
HQIV slot carries residual to fourth power through $m_e$.

Half-life follows \hyperref[lean:half-life-from-width]{half-life from width}:
$\tau_{1/2}=\ln 2/\Gamma$.

\paragraph{Width is the primary observable; $\tau$ is integrated.}
For allowed $\beta^-$ decay the phase-space factor rises steeply with endpoint energy:
$\Gamma(Q)\propto Q^5$ at leading order. A modest miss in width therefore appears
\emph{amplified} in clock time:
\[
  \frac{\Delta\tau}{\tau}\approx -\frac{\Delta\Gamma}{\Gamma},
  \qquad
  \frac{\Delta Q_{\mathrm{phase}}}{Q_{\mathrm{phase}}}\approx \frac{1}{5}\,
    \ln\!\frac{\Gamma_{\mathrm{ref}}}{\Gamma_{\mathrm{pred}}}.
\]
HQIV reports both: $\tau_{1/2}$ for laboratory comparison and $\Gamma_{\beta^-}$ /
$Q_{\mathrm{phase}}$ for width-ledger accuracy (\S\ref{sec:phenomenology}).

\paragraph{Neutron magnetic moment (weak-magnetism slot, not Zeeman).}
The neutron magnetic moment $\mu_n=-1.913\,\mu_N$ does \textbf{not} supply
$\sim 10\,\mathrm{keV}$ in laboratory trap fields ($|\mu_n|B$ is neV scale at
typical bottle fields). It enters instead through \emph{weak magnetism} and recoil
corrections to the $\beta$ width kernel---naturally at the per-mille to few-keV
scale on $Q_{\mathrm{phase}}$. The dominant free-branch offset is now booked by
local-curvature neutrino-opacity catalysis (\S\ref{sec:neutrino-opacity}); weak
magnetism and radiative normalization remain inside the monogamy envelope
$\pm\gamma/5$ on the width factor, not as a separate fit knob.

\paragraph{Spin-statistics: why $\tau_n\sim 880\,\mathrm{s}$ is not instantaneous.}
Neutrons are half-integer lattice modes
(\hyperref[lean:spin-stats]{spin--statistics module}): identical modes carry exchange
phase $-1$, so two free neutrons cannot occupy the same one-particle state or
``couple'' into a shared decaying composite without leaving the fermionic sector.
If such coupling \emph{were} allowed, decay would route through Ledger~I, not
Ledger~III: overlap energy $\Delta E\sim 1.17\,\mathrm{MeV}$ sets
$\Gamma_{\mathrm{strong}}=\Delta E/\hbar$ and
$t_{1/2}\sim 10^{-22}\,\mathrm{s}$
(\hyperref[lean:neutron-scaffold]{neutron stability scaffold},
\hyperref[lean:spin-stats-half-life]{spin-statistics half-life}).
The observed laboratory lifetime is therefore evidence that the \emph{weak}
$G_F$ channel is active---the strong resonance line is quarantined in Lean and
does not set $\tau_n$. For bonded clusters ($A>1$) the valley geometry factor
adds the ``$+1$'' fermionic spin-statistics slot in the exponent
(\S\ref{sec:valley}), suppressing weak tipping relative to the free branch; this
is the same Pauli/meta ledger that odd--odd nuclei book in \texttt{HQIVNuclei}.

\item[\textbf{Mass well.}]
  Symmetric per-nucleon embedding

  the same depth is subtracted from $p$ and $n$ so

\subsection{Condensed-phase geometry as local curvature density}
\label{sec:phase-geometry-density}

The same outside-contact primitive (\S\ref{sec:molecular-host}) extends to

covalent bond lengths, intermolecular contact $\xi$, and cohesive energies---fix
a \textbf{preferred allotrope orientation} (e.g.\ ice Ih for $\mathrm{H_2O}$).

\[
  \rho_{\mathrm{mass}}
    = \frac{Z\,M}{N_A\,V_{\mathrm{cell}}},
  \qquad
  V_{\mathrm{hex}} = \frac{\sqrt3}{2}\,a^2 c,
\]
and a dimensionless \textbf{curvature-density fraction}
\[
  \rho_{\mathrm{curv}}
    = \mathrm{clamp}_{[0,1]}\!\left(\frac{\rho_{\mathrm{mass}}}{\rho_{\mathrm{liquid,ref}}}\right),
\]
relative to a liquid reference at the melt comparison ($\rho_{\mathrm{liquid,ref}}=1.0\,\mathrm{g\,cm^{-3}}$

(\hyperref[lean:homogeneous-curvature]{homogeneous-curvature second order}):

  \qquad
  B_{\mathrm{eff}} = B_{\mathrm{hom}} + \delta B_{\mathrm{defect}},

Bulk $\mathrm{H_2O}$ ice Ih at 273\,K uses the experimental hexagonal cell

\paragraph{Bottle versus beam tension (interpretive).}
The world-average tension is real: representative bottle
$\tau_n^{\mathrm{bottle}}\approx 878\,\mathrm{s}$ vs beam
$\tau_n^{\mathrm{beam}}\approx 888\,\mathrm{s}$ ($\sim 1.2\%$, $\sim 10\,\mathrm{s}$,
$\sim 1.2\times 10^4\,\mathrm{ppm}$). HQIV does \textbf{not} claim to close this split
with temperature/gravity witnesses alone; the common free-branch width ledger is now
central at $\tau_n\approx 880\,\mathrm{s}$ with envelope $875$--$884\,\mathrm{s}$
(Table~\ref{tab:neutron-width}), bracketing both representatives without method knobs.
Substantive setup differences already named in the library:

\begin{description}[leftmargin=2.5em,style=nextline]
\item[\textbf{Bottle (UCN trap).}]
  Neutrons live in a \emph{confined embedding}: magnetic/gravity bottle, material
  guide boundaries, cryogenic storage (often $\sim 4\,\mathrm{K}$). The stored
  sample is a dilute \emph{fermionic gas}: Pauli exclusion forbids identical
  neutrons from coupling into a composite that would open the strong
  ($\Gamma\sim\Delta E/\hbar$) line (\S\ref{sec:weak-width}); each decay is still
  an independent weak $\beta^-$ event. What \emph{does} differ from beam is trap
  embedding---repeated boundary contacts book additional outside curvature (same
  bond-state contact primitive as \S\ref{sec:molecular-host}), magnetized bottles
  select spin states, and trap-induced \emph{decoherence} is an open slot on the
  weak Fano/Hopf bridge (\S\ref{sec:fano-hopf}). Standard experimental systematics
  (upscattering, wall losses) also lower the \emph{apparent} bottle count.
\item[\textbf{Beam (decay in flight).}]
  Free-branch $\beta^-$ with directed \emph{transport coherence} along the beam pipe;
  room-temperature beam line; effectively single-particle decay with no trap
  ensemble or wall-exchange ledger. The neutron samples lock-in-calibrated
  $\Omega_k$ readout more directly; co-spin horizon Doppler from beam velocity
  ($v/c\sim 10^{-5}$--$10^{-6}$) is an additive lapse slot in the flyby readout
  \cite{hqiv-flyby-paper} (ppm class, not percent).
\item[\textbf{Spin-statistics is not the bottle--beam split.}]
  Neither method accelerates $\beta$ decay toward the strong-width line: coupling is
  forbidden for spin-$1/2$ neutrons in both setups. The $\sim 1.2\%$ tension is
  therefore not ``instant decay if coupled'' but trap embedding + transport
  coherence + loss channels---orthogonal to the central width readout in
  Table~\ref{tab:neutron-width}.
\item[\textbf{What current witnesses already book.}]
  Temperature-only outside support from $4\,\mathrm{K}$ (bottle class) vs
  $300\,\mathrm{K}$ (beam class) is $\sim 10^2\,\mathrm{ppm}$ on $\tau_n$---far below
  the $\sim 1.2\%$ split. Gravity and molecular-host ledgers are likewise sub-percent
  for free neutrons.
\item[\textbf{Directional expectation (not fitted).}]
  Beam $\tau >$ bottle $\tau$ is consistent with: (i)~free-branch vs trap embedding;
  (ii)~higher effective transport coherence on the weak bridge in beam;
  (iii)~bottle loss channels. Closing the $\sim 10\,\mathrm{s}$ gap requires discharging
  the trap-embedding and coherence slots quantitatively---a named open task, not an
  ad hoc method knob.
\end{description}
