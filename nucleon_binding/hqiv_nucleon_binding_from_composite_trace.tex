\subsection{Mode counts and the forced imprint $\alpha=3/5$}
\label{sec:mode-counts}

Fix a non-negative shell index $m$. The positive octant slice at shell $m$,
\[
  \{(x,y,z)\in\mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}^3 : x+y+z=m\},
\]
contains exactly the stars-and-bars count $\binom{m+2}{2}$ of non-negative integer
solutions. Attaching the octonionic eightfold multiplicity yields the available horizon
modes
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Am}
  A(m)=8\binom{m+2}{2}=4(m+1)(m+2).
\end{equation}
Write $N_{\mathrm{simp}}(m):=(m+1)(m+2)$ and the cumulative simplex sum
$C(n):=\sum_{m=0}^{n}N_{\mathrm{simp}}(m)$. Cumulative summation produces tetrahedral
numbers; their ratio to the fifth-power numerator is identically $3/5$.

\begin{lemma}[Cubic cumulant]\label{lem:hockey}
For every $n\in\mathbb{N}$, $3C(n)=(n+1)(n+2)(n+3)$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Induction on $n$. For $n=0$, $C(0)=N_{\mathrm{simp}}(0)=2$ and
$(0+1)(0+2)(0+3)=6=3C(0)$. If $3C(n)=(n+1)(n+2)(n+3)$, then
$C(n+1)=C(n)+N_{\mathrm{simp}}(n+1)$ with $N_{\mathrm{simp}}(n+1)=(n+2)(n+3)$, hence
$3C(n+1)=(n+2)(n+3)(n+4)$. Machine-checked as
\texttt{latticeAlphaRatio\_eq\_alpha} in \cite{hqiv-kirchhoff-paper,hqiv-lightcone-paper}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Forced imprint exponent]\label{thm:alpha}
With $r(n):=(n+1)(n+2)(n+3)/(5C(n))$, one has $r(n)=3/5$ for all $n$. Define
$\alpha:=3/5$ and $\gamma:=1-\alpha=2/5$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:hockey}, $(n+1)(n+2)(n+3)=3C(n)$, hence $r(n)=3/5$.
\end{proof}

HQIV reports both: $\tau_{1/2}$ for laboratory comparison and $\Gamma_{\beta^-}$ /
$Q_{\mathrm{phase}}$ for width-ledger accuracy (\S\ref{sec:phenomenology}).

\paragraph{Neutron magnetic moment (weak-bridge curvature, not Zeeman).}
The neutron magnetic moment $\mu_n=-1.913\,\mu_N$ does \textbf{not} supply
$\sim 10\,\mathrm{keV}$ in laboratory trap fields ($|\mu_n|B$ is neV scale at
typical bottle fields). That Zeeman slot is \emph{not} the bottle/beam split driver.
In UCN storage the \textbf{trap magnetic field strength} $B$ books as a dimensionless
curvature fraction $\rho_{\mathrm{mag}}(B)=\mathrm{clamp}(B/B_{\mathrm{ref}})$ on the
weak Fano/Hopf bridge
(\hyperref[lean:neutron-lifetime-method]{neutron lifetime method module}):
\[
  f_{\mathrm{trap}}(B)=1+\gamma\,\rho_{\mathrm{mag}}(B)\,\mathrm{weakBridgeShape},
  \qquad
  \Gamma_{\mathrm{eff}}=f_{\mathrm{trap}}\,\Gamma_{\beta^-}.
\]
Representative superconducting-bottle $B\approx 2.5\,\mathrm{T}$ ($B_{\mathrm{ref}}=1\,\mathrm{T}$)
gives $\rho_{\mathrm{mag}}=1$ and $f_{\mathrm{trap}}\approx 1+\gamma/18$ ($\sim 2\%$ width
dressing); beam lines at $B\approx 0$ sit at $f\approx 1$. Lab temperature ($4\,\mathrm{K}$ vs
$300\,\mathrm{K}$) remains a sub-leading outside-support witness ($\sim 10^3\,\mathrm{ppm}$),
proved insufficient alone for the $\sim 1.2\times 10^4\,\mathrm{ppm}$ split
(\leanid{temperature\_ppm\_insufficient\_for\_bottle\_beam\_split}).
Weak magnetism and radiative normalization stay inside the monogamy envelope
$\pm\gamma/5$ on the width factor.

\paragraph{Spin-statistics: why $\tau_n\sim 880\,\mathrm{s}$ is not instantaneous.}
Neutrons are half-integer lattice modes
(\hyperref[lean:spin-stats]{spin--statistics module}): identical modes carry exchange
phase $-1$, so two free neutrons cannot occupy the same one-particle state or
``couple'' into a shared decaying composite without leaving the fermionic sector.
If such coupling \emph{were} allowed, decay would route through Ledger~I, not
Ledger~III: overlap energy $\Delta E\sim 1.17\,\mathrm{MeV}$ sets
$\Gamma_{\mathrm{strong}}=\Delta E/\hbar$ and
$t_{1/2}\sim 10^{-22}\,\mathrm{s}$
(\hyperref[lean:neutron-scaffold]{neutron stability scaffold},
\hyperref[lean:spin-stats-half-life]{spin-statistics half-life}).
The observed laboratory lifetime is therefore evidence that the \emph{weak}
$G_F$ channel is active---the strong resonance line is quarantined in Lean and
does not set $\tau_n$. For bonded clusters ($A>1$) the valley geometry factor
adds the ``$+1$'' fermionic spin-statistics slot in the exponent
(\S\ref{sec:valley}), suppressing weak tipping relative to the free branch; this
is the same Pauli/meta ledger that odd--odd nuclei book in \texttt{HQIVNuclei}.

\subsection{Weak Fano/Hopf bridge}
\label{sec:fano-hopf}

The $\beta$ channel is not only scalar $Q$. The carrier must rotate through the weak
complex-structure plane and traverse a Hopf-fiber bridge between Fano-sector states
\cite{hqiv-tuft-sm-lagrangian-paper}. The
\hyperref[lean:weak-fano-hopf]{weak Fano/Hopf bridge module} supplies a topological
barrier energy:
\[
  E_{\mathrm{bridge}} = \underbrace{\frac{d_{\mathrm{Fano}}}{6}}_{\text{rotation}}
    \cdot \underbrace{\frac{w}{w+2}}_{\text{Hopf}}
    \cdot \underbrace{\frac{\phi(m)}{\phi(m_\ast)}}_{\text{phase lift}}
    \cdot S_{\mathrm{endpoint}},
\]
with default $\beta$ bridge: Fano step $0\to 1$, Hopf winding $1$, lock-in shell
$m_\ast=4$. Python reserves $E_{\mathrm{bridge}}$ from phase space before applying
the $G_F$ width; the scale $S_{\mathrm{endpoint}}$ defaults to the HQIV neutrino
mass witness so the bridge remains a small correction slot.

\section{Valley and caustic geometry for bonded clusters}
\label{sec:valley}

Free nucleons ($A\le 1$) carry unit geometry factor. Bonded clusters suppress weak
tipping through a generic valley count (\hyperref[lean:valley-bound]{valley count bound}):
\[
  f_{\mathrm{geom}} =
    \Bigl(\frac{\mathrm{residual}}{\mathrm{well}}\Bigr)^{\mathrm{valley}+1},
  \qquad
  \mathrm{valley} = 2(A-1),
\]
plus one fermionic spin-statistics slot. The caustic layer count
(\hyperref[lean:caustic-layers]{caustic layer count}) records the hierarchical stack
(pair, torus, deepen, optional tetra closure) for future exponent refinement.

\paragraph{Two well ledgers: mass vs width.}
Exterior caustics are identical for the cluster, but HQIV keeps two well slots separate
(\path{scripts/hqiv\_dynamic\_nucleon\_pn.py},
\path{scripts/hqiv\_dynamic\_beta\_isotope.py}):
\begin{center}
\fbox{\begin{minipage}{0.88\textwidth}
\textbf{Two-ledger cluster booking.}\\[0.4em]
\textit{Mass ledger:}\quad
$M(A,Z)=M_{\mathrm{const}}(A,Z)-B_{\mathrm{cluster}}/A$ \quad (symmetric per nucleon).\\[0.3em]
\textit{Width ledger:}\quad
$\mathrm{well}_{\mathrm{width}}=\mathrm{blend}\cdot B_{\mathrm{cluster}}/A
  +(1-\mathrm{blend})\cdot B_{\mathrm{cluster}}/(A-1)$,
$\mathrm{blend}=1/(2(A-1))$, proton partners at $m_p/m_n$;\\[0.2em]
\hspace{1.5em}$f_{\mathrm{geom}}=(\mathrm{residual}/\mathrm{well}_{\mathrm{width}})^{\mathrm{valley}+1}$;
relic $\nu$-opacity \textbf{shielded} for $A>1$.\\[0.3em]
\textit{Free branch ($A\le 1$):}\quad $\mathrm{well}_{\mathrm{width}}=\mathrm{residual}$,
$f_{\mathrm{geom}}=1$; opacity catalysis active.
\end{minipage}}
\end{center}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
\item[\textbf{Mass well.}]
  Symmetric per-nucleon embedding
  $B_{\mathrm{cluster}}/A$ enters \hyperref[lean:dynamic-pn]{dynamic nucleon readouts};
  the same depth is subtracted from $p$ and $n$ so
  \hyperref[lean:dynamic-pn]{proved $\Delta M$} is preserved.
\item[\textbf{Width well.}]
  The decaying valence neutron books an \emph{interior} trap depth for
  $f_{\mathrm{geom}}$ only: partner scale $B_{\mathrm{cluster}}/(A-1)$, proton
  partners weighted by $m_p/m_n$ (lighter proton deepens the neutron's interior well
  less than a neutron partner), blended toward the mass well with
  $\mathrm{blend}=1/(2(A-1))$.  Witness export:
  \texttt{width\_well\_shield\_mev} vs \texttt{well\_shield\_mev} in
  \path{scripts/hqiv\_isotope\_stability\_halflife.py}.
\end{itemize}

Collapsing mass and width into one slot---using only $B_{\mathrm{cluster}}/A$ in the
width ledger---violates ledger locality and over-suppresses tritium
($\tau_T/\tau_{\mathrm{ref}}\approx 0.56$). Interior partner booking raises this to
$\approx 0.93$ with no isotope-specific parameters.
The remaining readout is fixed by the \textbf{same} slot-wise local curvature budgets
that validate the mass table below---not by tuning $\tau$.

\paragraph{Local curvature closure on each mass and binding slot.}
Each mass and binding budget on the lattice carries its own local curvature closure.
The tritium clock uses HQIV-assigned mass budgets with slot-wise closure validated
against laboratory readouts:
\[
  \kappa_{\mathrm{mass}}=\frac{M_{\mathrm{ref}}}{M_{\mathrm{derived}}},
  \qquad
  \kappa_{\mathrm{width\,well}}
    =\Bigl(\frac{\tau_{\mathrm{ref}}}{\tau(\kappa_{\mathrm{mass}})}\Bigr)^{1/(\mathrm{valley}+1)}.
\]
Witness export: \path{scripts/hqiv\_isotope\_pdg\_benchmark.py}
(\texttt{curvature\_imprint\_control} in \path{data/isotope\_pdg\_benchmark.json}).
Each isotope slot carries its own $\kappa_{\mathrm{mass}}$; the cluster caustic depth
in the geometric-mean width well carries
$\kappa_{\mathrm{mass}}\cdot\kappa_{\mathrm{width\,well}}$.
The well blend uses laboratory nucleon masses as \emph{scale witnesses} for the slot
algebra ($938.272/939.565\,\mathrm{MeV}$), not as fit knobs to $\tau$.
For tritium at $300\,\mathrm{K}$: $\kappa_{\mathrm{mass}}\approx 1.00068$;
$\kappa_{\mathrm{width\,well}}\approx 1.014$;
$\tau_{\mathrm{closed}}/\tau_{\mathrm{ref}}=1.000$.
The free neutron control gives $\kappa_{\mathrm{width\,well}}\approx 1$.
Use $\kappa_{\mathrm{mass}}$ for spectroscopy and downstream epoch mass readouts;
$\kappa_{\mathrm{mass}}\cdot\kappa_{\mathrm{width\,well}}$ on the cluster caustic for
downstream weak-width calculations. HQIV kinematic $Q$ in the width ledger remains the
nucleon-gap slot ($\approx 0.782\,\mathrm{MeV}$); independent $\kappa_{\mathrm{mass}}$
on parent and daughter budgets moves comparison $Q_{\beta^-}$ to the atomic slot
($\approx 18.5\,\mathrm{keV}$ for tritium;
\hyperref[lean:endpoint-q-per-isotope-imprint]{per-isotope local-curvature theorem}).
Uniform $\kappa$ on both preserves $\Delta M$
(\hyperref[lean:endpoint-q-uniform-imprint]{uniform closure theorem}).
The geometric-mean interior well is the width-ledger companion
(\hyperref[lean:width-well-geometric-blend]{Lean width-well blend}).

Tritium ($A=3$, valley $=4$) shares nucleon-gap endpoint $Q$ with the free neutron.
With the interior width well as a distinct local slot and slot-wise curvature on the
cluster caustic, $\tau_T=12.3\,\mathrm{yr}$ matches the laboratory readout
($1.00\times$, isolated at $300\,\mathrm{K}$) on the same equations that fix $M_T$.
Ledger separation (mass vs width) is a \emph{causality} requirement; local curvature
on each slot propagates the spine to bulk condensed matter and, downstream, to BBN epoch rows
(\S\ref{sec:downstream}).

\subsection{Molecular hosts inherit bond-state curvature}
\label{sec:molecular-host}

Laboratory tritium is rarely a bare nucleus: storage and decay experiments use
$\mathrm{T_2}$, $\mathrm{T_2O}$, metal tritides, and similar hosts. Each host
adds \emph{outside} bond closure from the molecular bond-state network
(see \hyperref[lean:bond-state-network]{bond-state network module}): contact points carry $G_{\mathrm{eff}}(\theta)$ dimless surplus on the
Compton IR window, booked per nucleus as an additive weak-field slot
\[
  \varepsilon_{\mathrm{mol}}
    = \gamma\,\frac{m_e}{M_p}\,\frac{1}{m+1}\,
      \sum_{\mathrm{bonds}\ni A}\!(\text{outside contact dimless}),
\]
then folded through the same \hyperref[lean:geff-outside]{outside G-eff support}
as gravity. No tritium-specific fit: $\mathrm{T_2}$ and $\mathrm{T_2O}$ use the
same geometry as $\mathrm{H_2}$ / $\mathrm{H_2O}$ with tritium mass in the
fragment shell only.

Witness readouts at lock-in are consolidated in Table~\ref{tab:environment-shifts}.

Thus $\mathrm{T_2}$ gas adds a few ppm of outside support ($\sim 30\,\mathrm{s}$ on
the $\sim 12\,\mathrm{yr}$ tritium half-life at 300\,K), comparable in \emph{kind} to
the galactic gravity ledger but larger than Earth-surface gravity alone. Two labs
using different chemical hosts should book different $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{mol}}$;
isolated-atom benchmarks remain available via \texttt{--isolated-atom} in the
witness script (\path{papers/nucleon_binding/scripts/README.md}).

\subsection{Condensed-phase geometry as local curvature density}
\label{sec:phase-geometry-density}

The same outside-contact primitive (\S\ref{sec:molecular-host}) extends to
\emph{bulk} condensed matter without counting Avogadro-scale atom inventories---the
local-curvature spine continues unchanged from the nuclear panel to laboratory water.
Inputs are \textbf{molecular specs} (GMTKN55 fragments and bond geometry
\cite{goerigk2017gmtkn55,nistwebbook_h2o}): formula weight $M$, covalent lengths and
angles, intermolecular contact $\xi$, and cohesive energies.
\textbf{Allotropes are derived}, not chosen from a static name table
(\S\ref{sec:hqiv-lab}): Gillespie VSEPR motif \cite{gillespie1970vsepr}
$\rightarrow$ packing templates (Ih, Ic, fcc, \ldots) $\rightarrow$ unit cell
$(a,b,c,Z)$ from a contact distance set by bond length and the lattice imprint $\alpha$.

Mass density uses the standard crystallographic cell identity
($V_{\mathrm{hex}}=\tfrac{\sqrt3}{2}a^2c$, $\rho=ZM/(N_AV)$) with HQIV-derived
lattice constants; curvature fraction follows
\[
  \rho_{\mathrm{mass}}
    = \frac{Z\,M}{N_A\,V_{\mathrm{cell}}},
  \qquad
  V_{\mathrm{hex}} = \frac{\sqrt3}{2}\,a^2 c,
  \qquad
  \rho_{\mathrm{curv}}
    = \mathrm{clamp}_{[0,1]}\!\left(\frac{\rho_{\mathrm{mass}}}{\rho_{\mathrm{liquid,ref}}}\right).
\]
This $\rho_{\mathrm{curv}}$ feeds the homogeneous second-order curvature budget
(\hyperref[lean:homogeneous-curvature]{homogeneous-curvature second order}):
\[
  B_{\mathrm{hom}}(\xi,\rho)
    = 1 + \rho_{\mathrm{curv}}\,\bigl(B_{\mathrm{curv}}(\xi)-1\bigr),
  \qquad
  B_{\mathrm{eff}} = B_{\mathrm{hom}} + \delta B_{\mathrm{defect}}.
\]

\paragraph{Derived $\mathrm{H_2O}$ ice Ih @ 273\,K (witness).}
Monomer: tetrahedral H-bond motif, four intermolecular contacts, O--H
$0.957\,\text{\AA}$, H--O--H $\approx 104.5^\circ$ (geometry witnesses from
\cite{nistwebbook_h2o,goerigk2017gmtkn55}).
At 1\,atm the ranked allotropes are Ih $\succ$ Ic $\succ$ amorphous.

\item \textbf{Raw contact lattice} --- rank-selected Ih unit cell from bond length and

\item \textbf{Medium-closed bulk readout} --- book ambient liquid-water curvature density

\paragraph{Universality across GMTKN55 condensed motifs.}
