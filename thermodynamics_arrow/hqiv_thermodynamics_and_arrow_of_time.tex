$(\alpha,\gamma)$ split that Lean forces combinatorially.
\textbf{This note answers: given those inputs, what thermodynamic law statements and what
arrow-of-time statements are already theorems, and what is still programme or
comparison-layer physics?}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]

  Thermal equilibrium as equality of $T(m)$; finite-window first-law balance;
  nonnegative entropy-production proxy on $C_3$; explicit-Euler quadratic-energy
  non-increase under a CFL bound; eventual shell cooling below any $\varepsilon>0$
  (the \hyperref[lean:thermo-laws]{thermodynamic laws from ladder} module).

  Finite-shell energy density, $P=U/3$, $s=(4/3)U/T$, positivity of $s$ for $T>0$,
  and the Kirchhoff detailed-balance identity at each shell
  (the \hyperref[lean:blackbody-ladder]{horizon blackbody ladder} stack).

  Causal acyclicity and horizon-overlap monogamy are mathematical templates in
  \cite{hqiv-3d-growth-paper}; discrete heat supplies a proved one-way dissipation sign;

\item \textbf{Comparison layer only.}
  Continuum statistical mechanics, ergodicity, and textbook Boltzmann $H$-theorems.
\item \textbf{Not claimed.}
  Derivation of full interacting continuum thermodynamics from first principles in this
  note; uniqueness of all equilibrium states in field theory; microscopic time reversal

\section{The shell temperature ladder and equilibrium}
\label{sec:ladder}

\begin{definition}[Shell temperature readout]
\label{def:T-ladder}
The HQIV temperature ladder assigns to each shell index $m\in\mathbb{N}$ the
dimensionless readout
\[
  T(m) \;=\; \frac{1}{m+1}.
\]
\end{definition}

This is the same ordered field object used in horizon thermodynamics and epoch placement
throughout the library: larger $m$ means \emph{colder} shells in this convention, and the
map $m\mapsto T(m)$ is strictly decreasing.

\begin{definition}[Thermal equilibrium on shells]
\label{def:thermal-eq}
Shells $m$ and $n$ are in thermal equilibrium when $T(m)=T(n)$.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Zeroth law as an equivalence relation]
\label{prop:zeroth}
Equality of the readout $T(m)$ is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive on
$\mathbb{N}$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Machine-checked as the \hyperref[lean:zeroth-law]{zeroth-law cluster} in the thermodynamic
laws module.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Physical reading]
The zeroth law is not imported from continuum phenomenology: it is the statement that
``same ladder temperature'' is a consistent equilibrium label on the discrete index.
Coupling to dimensionful MeV or kelvin displays is a downstream calibration layer
(epoch modules, lock-in witnesses) and is not required for the relations in
Propositions~\ref{prop:zeroth}--\ref{prop:third}.
\end{remark}

\section{First, second, and third laws on the discrete spine}
\label{sec:laws}

\subsection{First law as finite-window redistribution}

The division-algebra/zeta scaffold proves a weighted conservation identity: a finite
window of shells redistributes ladder temperature so that shell weights sum to a fixed
reference value $T_{\mathrm{ref}}$ (the \hyperref[lean:first-law]{first-law finite-window balance}).

\begin{theorem}[First-law style balance]
\label{thm:first}
For every reference $T_{\mathrm{ref}}$, window size $N>0$, and profile parameter $p$,
\[
  \sum_{m<N} \mathrm{tempLadderConserved}(T_{\mathrm{ref}},m)\,
  \mathrm{dimShellWeight}(p,N,m)
  \;=\; T_{\mathrm{ref}}.
\]
\end{theorem}

This is the discrete analogue of ``internal energy accounting on a finite patch'': no
continuum density $\mathrm{d}U$ is assumed.

\subsection{Second law as a sign theorem on a toy heat graph}

To avoid hand-waving about dissipation, Lean carries a minimal periodic graph $C_3$ with
an explicit graph Laplacian $\Delta$ (the \hyperref[lean:toy-heat]{toy discrete heat}
module). For any state $u$ on the three nodes,
\[
  \sum_i u_i\,(\Delta u)_i \;=\; -\sum_i (u_i-u_{i+1})^2 \;\le\; 0,
\]
so the inner product of $u$ with its discrete Laplacian is never positive.

\begin{definition}[Entropy-production proxy on $C_3$]
\label{def:entropy-prod}
Define $\Sigma(u):=-\sum_i u_i(\Delta u)_i$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Second law: nonnegative production proxy]
\label{thm:second-sign}
For all $u$, $\Sigma(u)\ge 0$.
\end{theorem}

An explicit Euler update $u\mapsto u^+$ decreases the quadratic energy
$\sum_i u_i^2$ provided a CFL condition $3\,\nu\,\Delta t\le 2$ holds
(the \hyperref[lean:second-euler]{second-law Euler monotonicity} theorem).
This is a \emph{discrete} irreversibility witness: it does not appeal to a continuum
entropy functional whose existence is assumed.

\begin{remark}[Scope of the toy graph]
The $C_3$ heat model motivates fluid-closure and turbulence scaffolds elsewhere in the
repository; it is \textbf{not} a claim about 3D Navier--Stokes. Its role here is narrower:
fix the \emph{sign} of dissipation that the second law must inherit on a patch.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Third law as eventual ladder cooling}

\begin{theorem}[Third-law style eventual cooling]
\label{prop:third}
For every $\varepsilon>0$ there exists a shell $m$ with $T(m)<\varepsilon$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Since $T(m)=1/(m+1)\to 0$ along the index, the statement is pure archimedean order on
$\mathbb{R}$; see the \hyperref[lean:third-law]{third-law eventual cooling} theorem.
\end{proof}

\section{Finite-mode blackbody thermodynamics}
\label{sec:blackbody}

The Kirchhoff paper \cite{hqiv-kirchhoff-paper} constructs a finite Planck spectrum
between an ultraviolet shell $m_{\mathrm{UV}}$ and an infrared cutoff $m_{\mathrm{IR}}$.
The horizon blackbody ladder module packages standard \emph{readouts} on that truncated sum:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
\item photon number density as a finite shell sum;
\item radiation pressure $P=U/3$;
\item entropy density $s=(4/3)\,U/T$ for $T>0$;
\item detailed balance: emission equals absorption shell-wise
  (the \hyperref[lean:kirchhoff-balance]{Kirchhoff detailed-balance bundle}).
\end{itemize}

\begin{proposition}[Entropy density is nonnegative on the patch]
\label{prop:s-nonneg}
For $T>0$ and finite cutoffs, $s\ge 0$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[No ultraviolet catastrophe]
Because $\mathcal{N}(m)=(m+2)(m+1)$ is finite at each shell and only finitely many shells
enter a given readout, $U$ and $s$ are \emph{sums}, not integrals requiring
$\omega_{\max}\to\infty$.
Continuum Stefan--Boltzmann or Wien laws may be cited as calculation approximations for
comparison; they are not the proved objects.
\end{remark}

\section{The arrow of time}
\label{sec:arrow}

Textbook thermodynamics often \emph{defines} the arrow of time by the second law while
leaving the microscopic time-reversal symmetry of Hamiltonian mechanics untouched.
HQIV reverses the emphasis on the discrete spine: the arrow is a \textbf{readout} of three
already-audited structures.

\subsection{Causal direction on the null lattice}

The growth paper \cite{hqiv-3d-growth-paper} treats a locally finite causal partial order
$\prec$ on null data.
Acyclicity of influence is the operational content of ``forward in time'': no shell may
consume information from its own future cone without violating the poset.
Horizon overlap sets $O(e_i,e_j)$ support a subadditivity template (informational monogamy):
shared past/future cones cannot be double-counted without paying an overlap tax.
That template is the same accounting Brodie's thermodynamic horizon package
\cite{brodie2026} reads through Jacobson's flux--entropy identity \cite{Jacobson1995}.

\textbf{Lean status.} Monogamy is a proved \emph{template} on overlap functionals together
with the forced split $\gamma=1-\alpha$; specialising it to a unique continuum entropy
functional is not a theorem of this note.

\subsection{Thermodynamic direction from dissipation signs}

On the minimal heat patch, Theorem~\ref{thm:second-sign} fixes a direction for relaxation:
states with unequal edge jumps produce strictly positive $\Sigma(u)$ unless $u$ is a
spatially constant mode on $C_3$.
Combined with the Euler monotonicity witness, forward numerical evolution is a
\emph{contracting} readout in quadratic energy under the stated CFL bound.

\subsection{Cosmological direction: outward shell indexing}

Cosmology in HQIV is not a homogeneous FLRW continuum taken as fundamental; it is a
sequence of shell readouts with monotonic mode budgets.
The temperature ladder makes the thermodynamic arrow coincide with \textbf{increasing
shell index toward the present cold background}: larger $m$ $\Leftrightarrow$ smaller
$T(m)$.
The wall-clock versus apparent-age split \cite{hqiv-kirchhoff-paper,hqiv-oct-action-paper}
is an additional observer-sector arrow (lapse compression) orthogonal to the ladder sign
but compatible with it: both select a privileged ``now'' slice without introducing a
tunable entropy functional.

\subsection{Discrete phase evolution (programme layer)}

The parallel-Poincar\'e programme records one-step evolution on a 3-complex with an
optional ``extinction'' outcome and a Lyapunov functional that must not increase along
forward steps (the \hyperref[lean:phase-evolution]{discrete phase evolution} module).
Where a `NatLyapunovDescent` certificate is supplied, iteration terminates---a discrete
well-foundedness witness for approach to equilibrium on that patch.

\textbf{Status.} This cluster is load-bearing for the long-term Poincar\'e bridge but is
not yet bundled as a single umbrella theorem in the sense of the BBN vital readouts.
We list it here because it is the natural upgrade path from toy $C_3$ heat to
shell-resolved curvature flow.
